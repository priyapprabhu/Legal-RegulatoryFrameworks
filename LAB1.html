Details about Scope of GDPR:

GDPR applies to organizations within the EU and those outside the EU offering goods or services to or monitoring the behavior of EU residents. It covers processing of personal data by automated means and manual processing if part of a filing system.

Details about Objective of GDPR:

  • To protect the personal data and privacy of EU residents.
  • To harmonize data privacy laws across Europe, ensuring consistent protection for all individuals within the EU.

Details about Rights for Individuals in GDPR:

  • Right to Access: Obtain access to personal data and information about its processing.
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of personal data under certain conditions.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Limit processing of personal data in specific situations.
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive personal data in a portable format and transfer it to another controller.
  • Right to Object: Object to processing of personal data, including for direct marketing.

Details about Rights for Organizations in GDPR:

  • Lawful Processing: Ensure all data processing activities are based on lawful grounds.
  • Data Protection by Design and Default: Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure data protection principles are met.
  • Data Breach Notifications: Notify supervisory authorities and affected individuals of data breaches within specified timeframes.
  • Appointing Data Protection Officers (DPOs): Designate a DPO for organizations involved in large-scale data processing.

Details about Consequences of GDPR:

  • Fines and Penalties: Non-compliance can result in fines up to 20 million euros or 4% of the annual global turnover.
  • Reputation Damage: Breaches and non-compliance can lead to significant reputational harm.
  • Legal Action: Data subjects can seek compensation for damages resulting from GDPR violations.

Details about Scope of HIPAA:

HIPAA applies to organizations within the US. It covers electronic protected health information (ePHI) and includes healthcare providers, health plans, and healthcare clearinghouses.

Details about Objective of HIPAA:

  • To safeguard the privacy and security of individuals' health information.
  • To provide individuals with rights regarding their health information while ensuring that healthcare organizations maintain high standards of data security and confidentiality.

Details about Rights for Individuals in HIPAA:

  • Right to Access: Obtain copies of their health records and request amendments.
  • Right to Privacy: Control over the use and disclosure of their health information.
  • Right to Notification: Receive notifications about privacy practices and any breaches of their health information.
  • Right to Request Restrictions: Request limitations on the use and disclosure of their PHI.

Details about Rights for Organizations in HIPAA:

  • Privacy Rule Compliance: Implement safeguards to protect the PHI and ensure that only authorized individuals can access it.
  • Security Rule Compliance: Establish security measures to protect ePHI, including administrative, physical, and technical safeguards.
  • Breach Notifications: Notify affected individuals and the Department of Health and Human Services of any breaches of unsecured PHI.
  • Training and Policies: Develop and enforce policies and procedures to ensure compliance with HIPAA regulations and train staff accordingly.

Details about Consequences of HIPAA:

  • Fines and Penalties: Non-compliance can result in fines ranging from $100 to $50,000 per violation, with a maximum annual penalty of $1.5 million.
  • Criminal Penalties: Severe violations can lead to criminal charges with penalties including imprisonment.
  • Reputation Damage: Breaches and non-compliance can damage the organization's reputation and trustworthiness.

Details about Scope of CCPA:

The CCPA applies to for-profit businesses that collect personal data from California residents, do business in California, and meet certain thresholds (e.g., revenue or data volume).

Details about Objective of CCPA:

  • To enhance privacy rights and consumer protection for residents of California.
  • To provide consumers with more control over their personal data and how it is collected and used by businesses.

Details about Rights for Individuals in CCPA:

  • Right to Know: Access the personal data a business collects about them.
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of their personal data collected by businesses.
  • Right to Opt-Out: Opt-out of the sale of their personal data to third parties.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: Not be discriminated against for exercising their privacy rights under CCPA.

Details about Rights for Organizations in CCPA:

  • Data Collection and Disclosure: Inform consumers about the categories of personal data collected and the purposes for which it is used.
  • Consumer Requests: Implement processes to handle consumer requests related to data access, deletion, and opt-out.
  • Privacy Notices: Update privacy policies and notices to comply with CCPA requirements.
  • Data Security: Ensure adequate security measures to protect personal data.

Details about Consequences of CCPA:

  • Fines and Penalties: Violations can result in fines of up to $2,500 per violation and $7,500 for intentional violations.
  • Private Lawsuits: Consumers can seek damages for certain violations, including unauthorized access or disclosure of personal data.
  • Reputation Damage: Non-compliance can harm an organization's reputation and customer trust.

Details about Scope of PCI-DSS:

PCI-DSS applies to any organization that processes, stores, or transmits credit card information, regardless of size or transaction volume.

Details about Objective of PCI-DSS:

  • To protect cardholder data from theft and unauthorized access.
  • To ensure that organizations maintain a secure environment for handling payment card data.

Details about Rights for Individuals in PCI-DSS:

  • Right to Security: Assurance that their payment card data is protected and secure.
  • Right to Privacy: Protection of personal information from unauthorized access or misuse.
  • Right to Redress: Ability to dispute fraudulent charges and seek resolution.

Details about Rights for Organizations in PCI-DSS:

  • Compliance: Follow PCI-DSS requirements to ensure the security of payment card data.
  • Regular Assessments: Conduct regular security assessments and vulnerability scans.
  • Incident Response: Have an incident response plan in place to address security breaches.

Details about Consequences of PCI-DSS:

  • Fines and Penalties: Non-compliance can result in fines from card networks and banks.
  • Reputation Damage: Breaches and non-compliance can damage an organization's reputation.
  • Increased Costs: Organizations may face increased costs for remediation and mitigation.

Details about Scope of FISMA:

FISMA applies to federal agencies and their contractors that manage federal information systems. It covers all information systems used or operated by federal agencies, including those used by contractors.

Details about Objective of FISMA:

  • To protect federal information and information systems from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.
  • To ensure the availability, integrity, and confidentiality of federal information systems.

Details about Rights for Individuals in FISMA:

  • Right to Privacy: Expectation of privacy for personal information handled by federal agencies.
  • Right to Security: Assurance that personal and sensitive information is protected.

Details about Rights for Organizations in FISMA:

  • Compliance: Ensure compliance with FISMA requirements for information security.
  • Security Plans: Develop and maintain information security plans for federal systems.
  • Regular Assessments: Conduct regular security assessments and audits of federal information systems.

Details about Consequences of FISMA:

  • Fines and Penalties: Non-compliance may lead to legal consequences and penalties.
  • Increased Scrutiny: Agencies may face increased oversight and audits.
  • Operational Impact: Non-compliance can disrupt operations and impact agency functions.

Details about Scope of PDPA:

PDPA applies to organizations operating within Singapore and those outside Singapore that handle personal data of individuals residing in Singapore. It covers personal data collected, used or disclosed by organizations in both electronic and non-electronic forms.

Details about Objective of PDPA:

  • To protect individuals' personal data and ensure that organizations handle personal data responsibly and transparently.
  • To provide a framework for the collection, use and disclosure of personal data that respects individuals' privacy rights.

Details about Rights for Individuals in PDPA:

  • Right to Access:Request access to personal data held by organizations and obtain information about its use.
  • Right to Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Right to Consent: Control over how personal data is collected, used and disclosed.
  • Right to Withdrawal: Withdraw consent for the collection, use or disclosure of personal data at any time.

Details about Rights for Organizations in PDPA:

  • Compliance Requirements: Implement policies and practices to ensure compliance with PDPA principles, including data protection, consent, and retention policies.
  • Data Protection Officer: Appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO) to oversee compliance and address data protection concerns.
  • Data Breach Notifications: Notify the Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC) and affected individuals of data breaches that pose a significant risk.

Details about Consequences of PDPA:

  • Fines and Penalties: Non-compliance can result in fines up to $1 million or higher, depending on the severity of the violation.
  • Reputation Damage: Non-compliance can lead to loss of consumer trust and harm the organization’s reputation.
  • Legal Action: Organizations may face legal action from affected individuals or enforcement actions by the PDPC.

Details about Scope of LGPD:

LGPD applies to organizations operating within Brazil and those outside Brazil that process personal data of individuals located in Brazil.

Details about Objective of LGPD:

  • To protect the personal data of individuals and regulate how organizations collect, use and manage such data.
  • To establish a framework for data protection that aligns with international standards and promotes transparency and accountability.

Details about Rights for Individuals in LGPD:

  • Right to Access: Obtain information about the data collected, its processing, and its purposes.
  • Right to Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Right to Deletion: Request the deletion of personal data when it is no longer necessary or upon withdrawal of consent.
  • Right to Data Portability: Limit processing of personal data in specific situations.
  • Right to Opposition Object to the processing of personal data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Revoke Consent Withdraw consent for data processing at any time.

Details about Rights for Organizations in LGPD:

  • ComplianceImplement measures to ensure compliance with LGPD, including data protection policies, risk assessments, and security measures.
  • Data Protection Officer: Appoint a DPO to oversee data protection practices and act as a point of contact for data subjects and the National Data Protection Authority (ANPD).
  • Data Breach Notifications: Report data breaches to the ANPD and affected individuals within specified timeframes.

Details about Consequences of LGPD:

  • Fines and Penalties: Non-compliance can result in fines up to 2% of the organization's revenue in Brazil, capped at R$50 million per violation.
  • Reputation Damage: Failure to comply can lead to significant damage to the organization’s reputation and loss of consumer trust.
  • Legal Action: Organizations may face legal action from individuals or enforcement actions from the ANPD.

Details about Scope of NIST:

NIST applies to organizations operating in the US primarily but widely adopted globally as a benchmark for information security. It covers a range of standards and guidelines for securing information systems and data including risk management, cybersecurity frameworks and security controls.

Details about Objective of NIST:

  • To provide guidelines and frameworks for enhancing cybersecurity and managing information security risks.
  • To establish standards that help organizations protect sensitive data and ensure the integrity, availability, and confidentiality of their information systems.

Details about Rights for Individuals in NIST:

  • Security Assurance: NIST standards help ensure that individuals' personal data is protected through effective security measures, though they do not directly grant rights to individuals.
  • Transparency: Individuals can benefit from improved security practices that protect their data and privacy, indirectly supporting their rights.

Details about Rights for Organizations in NIST:

  • Implementation Guidance: Access to comprehensive guidelines for implementing robust security measures, including risk assessments and security controls.
  • Frameworks: Utilize established frameworks such as the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) to manage and reduce cybersecurity risks effectively.
  • Compliance: Align with industry best practices and regulatory requirements to enhance overall security posture and resilience.

Details about Consequences of NIST:

  • Regulatory Penalties: Non-compliance with specific regulatory requirements that reference NIST standards can result in fines and legal consequences.
  • Reputation Damage: Inadequate implementation of NIST standards can harm an organization’s reputation and undermine trust with stakeholders and customers.
  • Operational Impact:Failure to adhere to NIST standards may lead to increased vulnerability to cyber threats and security breaches.
